REAL-WORLD ENGINEERING FIXES

A high-speed diverter module kept failing in the field — repeated re-torque checks, angular drift, and vibration issues, even with correct specs.

Without changing the design, we solved the root cause: sleeve misalignment during final assembly. By adding an axial stop, tightening coaxial tolerance to ±0.01 mm, and improving the fit geometry, the OEM cut shift-related failures by over 80% — restoring runtime stability and long-term joint reliability.
